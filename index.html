Been There

</h3>

Been There is the map app for people who are bursting with travel recommendations (such as that amazing, little dumpling place on the top floor of the Wudaokou Clothing Market in Beijing). Add all of your favourite travel places (and their details) to your very own map and then easily share them with friends and family.

Technology: React.js, Node.js, Express, MongoDB & Google Maps API.

<h3>

How Do You Doodle?

</h3>

An online, two-player drawing and guessing game (hmmm... sounds a bit like Pictionary). Player 1 is the drawer and can use either the mouse or a Leap Motion controller to create their artwork. Player 2, the guesser, can watch the drawer's sketch appear on their screen in real-time (thanks to the magic of WebSockets). A gallery of How Do You Doodle? artworks is also included for players and passers-by to check out.

Technology: JavaScript, Ruby on Rails, HTML5 Canvas & WebSockets with ActionCable.

</h3>

A curated web store that sells treasures found on the gigantic Chinese ecommerce site Taobao. Each month eight thoughtfully selected and translated items from Chinese independent designers appear on the site with the aim of promoting Chinese design to a Western market.

<h3>

Tic-Tac-Toe

</h3>

The classic tic-tac-toe game remodelled into an online language-learning tool for reviewing those notoriously similar pairs of Chinese characters. Each game is randomly assigned a pair of tricky Chinese characters which serve as the players' icons (because X and O are so 2005). Players view their characters' details (including pronunciation and definition) on a flashcard before starting the game. Cheat buttons are also included which briefly highlights a player's past moves.

Technology: HTML, CSS, JavaScript & jQuery.
